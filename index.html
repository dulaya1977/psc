Integrated PSC Pre-Arrival Checklist

This ultimate checklist combines historical PSC detention records with ClassNK requirements. Walk to the sites where the devices and equipment are located and conduct functional testing right away.

Detailed Functional Check & Actions Required Result Remarks / Required Data
1. SMS, Documents & Personnel (Navigation, Publications & Training)
Conduct risk assessments and report any critical equipment damage per SMS; implement temporary measures.
Ensure closed-loop corrective records for all historical deficiencies (e.g., OWS 15ppm, sewage high-level alarms).
Ensure Critical Equipment records (WMC-20, 21), pre-work permits, Loading Plans, and BW logs are updated and available.
Conduct practical drills for new systems like Shaft Power Limitation (ShaPoLi).
Check all Seafarers' Employment Agreements (SEA) for contract expiry dates and paid annual leave quotas.
Ensure ECDIS familiarization training is completed and logged.
Ensure crew's rest hour records exactly match actual activities.
Verify 15-minute fire patrols reflect on watch schedules and rest hour records.
Display "Working Language Is English" note in Deck and Engine log books.
Verify inventory of medical oxygen bottles (40L, 20L, 10L, 2L); check if dangerous cargo loaded.
Ensure medical chest certificate is valid and record expiry date.
Verify ship type ("Bulk carrier") is correct on SMC/ISSC and Executive Summary Report (ESP) is onboard.
Charts and publications for current and intended voyage are available onboard.
NTM (Notice to Mariner) is up to date. (Fill in Last NTM No.)
Charts and admiralty publications (e.g., Sailing Directions, List of Lights) are corrected up to date. Temporary and preliminary corrections are NOT missing.
Passage plan takes into account environmental protection measures, No-Go areas, and is made on a berth-to-berth basis.
More than one method used for fixing vessel's position during pilotage (evidence must be available).
Navigation officers are familiar with setting up AIS info and operation of NUC Light.
Auto-pilot tested by manual operation at least once a watch.
Compass error determined at least once a watch.
Duty officer understands how to set up radar reflector.
Officers are familiar with daylight signal operation; check voltage of battery for daylight signal.
Inmarsat C set up to receive maritime safety information (EGC) for the operational area.
GMDSS equipment tested and recorded in GMDSS log book. GMDSS reserved power (24V) is NOT defective.
Australian Seafarer's Handbook (AHP20) is on board. (If calling Australia)
Navigation officers familiar with light/shape signals of Japan Maritime Safety Laws. (If calling Japan)
Vessel submitted ballast water report to NBIC at least 24 hours before arrival. (If calling USA)
SDS (Safety Data Sheet) is available for onboard cargo.
Crew member not provided with a copy on board complaint procedures; verify crew is conversant with procedures.
2. Engine Room, Machinery & Pollution Control
Thoroughly clean E/R bilges, stern tube drip trays, and purifier areas. Unblock purifier oil drain pipes.
Strictly remove all unapproved plastic buckets and rubber hoses used to drain oil.
Repair main and auxiliary engine oil leaks immediately (e.g., generator lube oil leaking from pressure gauge).
Check high-pressure fuel pipe leak alarms. Ensure in-use fuel sulfur content complies with regulations (<0.5%).
Repair defective ballast pumps or evaluate for downgrade per SMS.
All engine officers must practice obtaining the boiler water level in the gauge glass.
Test steering gear alarms. Conduct an emergency steering time test (MUST be ≤ 28 seconds).
All engine crew must complete training and practice the emergency steering procedure. Deck officers aware of pre-departure test.
Conduct 15ppm separator alarming test; ensure auto-stop device activates within 20 seconds.
Ensure the bypass OWS overboard discharge valve is physically "Close".
Solenoid valve of oily water separator is functioning well.
Crew able to demonstrate test of low level alarm of steering gear lub-oil tanks. Lub oil storage tank reached minimum level.
Self-closing device of oil level gauge MUST NOT be secured by wire in any position.
Rubber mats are provided in front of electrical panels (main/emergency switchboard). Low insulation checked.
Oil drums are properly stowed away from heat sources (e.g., Incinerator).
Oil Record Book is properly filled. Weekly recordings of sludge tanks made according to IOPP Cert. Fuel oil changeover procedures recorded correctly.
EIAPP Certificates for auxiliary engines are available. NOx Technical File properly recorded after replacing major spare parts.
Battery room contains no unserviced lead batteries on floor; no leaking observed. Maintained sufficient liquid.
Temperature gauges on auxiliary engine cooling water and other gauges in machinery space are NOT broken/malfunctioning.
Pipes in engine room are NOT patched without permanent repair.
Cleanliness in engine room improved. No oily rags found scattered in E/R.
Insulation materials are fitted on aux boiler and the exhaust pipe of emergency generator.
Spark protecting steel nets inside air vent heads for LO and FO tanks are NOT missing or completely corroded.
Oil mist detector for crank case is functioning normally.
3. Fire Fighting Systems
Keep emergency pump room dry; motor insulation must be >0 MΩ.
Conduct a loaded running test for main/emergency fire pumps; record outlet pressure in MPa. Pump has NO serious leakage.
Replace leaking fire main joints and hoses. Test remote start of main fire pump.
Inspect fire hoses/boxes (no cracks); check nozzle gaskets are not aged, cracked, or wasted.
Check CO2 cylinder pressure, door alarms, and arrangement compliance.
Safety pins MUST be removed from fixed CO2 systems. Crew familiarized with simulation release and timing of opening/closing isolation valve.
Never intentionally shut off water-based systems. Ensure it is set to automatic mode, not manual.
Test stopping of power ventilation from outside. Test quick-closing valves for ME/generators.
Test all fire door self-closers and remove any ropes/hooks holding doors open. Ensure doors close tightly.
Check fire penetrations and clear E/R escape routes. Record simulation escape from E/R with EEBD.
Mark open/close status on funnel fire dampers. Comprehensive inspection on E/R fan dampers. Ensure dampers can be closed fully.
Foam applicator ready in E/R. Ensure SCBA/EEBD fully charged. Check fireman outfits are not damaged.
Post correct fire control plan symbol at gangways and update crew list. Fire control plan kept inside watertight enclosure.
Crew familiar with duties in fire drill.
SOLAS training manual is ship specific (maker's instructions of each LSA and FFA are included).
Priming pump water reservoir on emergency fire pump is NOT corroded or leaking.
A-60 insulation on forward bulkhead in engine room is NOT damaged.
Local fire-fighting system panel on bridge does NOT indicate abnormal (low water level alarm). Fresh water supply valve is OPEN.
Crew familiar with fire-fighting requirement for helicopter landing; specific equipment available.
Fire hose in portable foam applicator box next to E/R boiler is NOT defective.
Fire detection and alarm system is operative. Detectors are NOT covered by plastic bags.
Specific testing equipment for fire detectors (heat and smoke) is available.
Portable fire extinguisher is kept near the entrance of each designated space.
Air reservoir for F.O and D.O tanks quick shut off valve is NOT in low pressure condition.
The key for releasing CO2 during emergency is available inside the CO2 room.
4. Life Saving & Emergency Systems
Test dual battery engine starts (No.1 & No.2) and run engines. Replace burst hydraulic pipes and cracked ladder steps.
Verify LB cradle/davit has no cracks, limit switches work, and release gear is reset. Move davit.
Remove Fall Preventer Devices (FPDs) immediately before berthing.
Check hydrostatic interlocks (free to move). Check LB windows & seat belts. Remote control wire inside both lifeboats ready.
Rescue boat davit operable in "dead ship condition" (manual backup). Manuals readable/illuminated.
Clear liferaft embarkation routes and lubricate release. Ensure HRU/liferaft annual service is valid.
Ensure portable lights are provided for the bow liferaft. Check retro-reflective tapes.
Inventory and replace expired LSA and 2-way VHF batteries. Sea sickness pills not expired.
Conduct immersion suit donning practice. Ensure suits fit correctly, no damage, zippers waxed. Air pressure test record available.
Test emergency generator auto-start and load-sharing. Check periodic testing record.
Ensure the diesel oil tank for the emergency generator is full or sufficient.
Confirm the emergency generator connects automatically to the switchboard. Emergency generator able to start by second battery set.
Conduct night rounds to check all emergency lights. Ensure bulbs operative and covers undamaged.
Lifeboat drill as required (e.g., 6-monthly freefall simulation, 3-monthly maneuvering in water).
Crew familiar with operating rescue boat davit to swing out by hydraulic accumulator.
Crew familiar with operating release hook of rescue boat (auto and manual release).
Liferaft properly stowed and painter correctly connected to HRU weak link. Liferaft launching instruction visible.
Liferaft embarkation ladder shackle pins NOT seized.
MOB lifebuoy on bridge wings has sufficient weight for quick release. Releasing mechanism NOT stuck. Marks/color NOT faded.
Lifebuoy is NOT missing in its legal position required on life saving plan.
Steering gear system operative on lifeboat. Instructions for release hooks available inside.
Freefall lifeboat inventories secured and stored in designated locker.
Rescue boat is ready for immediate use (NOT covered by canvas). Search light NOT defective.
Rescue boat running test done correctly with cooling system device to prevent overheat. Boat hook connected.
Rescue boat fuel tank is FULL (not just 25%) and sufficient spare fuels available.
Interlock system of release lever for lifeboats correctly set by maker's guidance. Search light & canopy light functioning.
5. Structure, Weathertightness & Safety Culture
Replace aged hatch packings, drain valves, and securing devices (wing nuts, toggle screws). Check coamings for corrosion.
Check all cleats; ensure rubber packing has no missing sections, deep cracks, or depressions.
Hammer-test air pipe heads and natural ventilators; ensure float balls move freely.
Inspect ventilation closing devices indicated on the Fire Control Plan.
Inspect/repair hull holes and severe rust; test WT/WTD doors. Repaint freeboard/deck lines to contrast color.
Post the enclosed space list (WMR-OT-01) in mess rooms and offices. Crew familiar with entry procedure.
Post "Unauthorized Entry" signage and apply appropriate locking methods to enclosed spaces.
Check gas detector calibration intervals (6/12 months), working order, and span gas availability.
Ensure crew wears PPE; engine crew must wear hearing protection (notice posted at E/R entrance).
Verify fire curtains, insulation mats (switchboards), covered turbochargers, pump meshes in place.
Test the ship alarm and public addressing system. Secure heavy objects in bosun store.
Keep access clear: fire hydrants, bosun store extinguisher, CO2 room, anchor chain bitters.
Electrical socket in crew mess room and cabins are NOT in hazardous condition.
Plugs on oil pans on main deck are NOT missing.
Operation platform for windlass is NOT heavily rusted. Rollers are NOT seized on forecastle deck.
Mooring rope in use is NOT in poor condition. Mooring lines fastened on bollard, not warping drums.
Anchor is flushed and cleaned.
Hydraulic jacks for hatch covers are NOT leaking.
Sounding pipe covers are NOT missing. Automatic closing devices on sounding pipes not seized open.
Skylight of engine room is watertight.
Pilot ladders are NOT defective. Handrails and safety net on accommodation ladder NOT damaged.
National flags flying onboard are in good condition.
Deck tools for emergency towing are collected in specific position and ready. Booklets in required locations.
Warning sign for cargo operation restricted areas is set during cargo operation.
NO unsafe working at height/unguarded hatch cover openings during cleaning. Personal safety equip used.
Water ingress alarm system is NOT kept switched off. Crew familiar with system. Remote control of forecastle dewatering works.
6. Navigation Equipment
Replace damaged light casings and paint side light screens matt black. Inspect all lights with housing.
Inspect Day Shapes and verify the required inventory (two balls, a cylinder, one/two cones, and a diamond or two cones joined at bases). Measure their diameters to ensure they are not less than 0.6 meters in diameter.
Verify no bubbles in compasses; log observed errors. Synchronize radar/gyro headings. Wing rudder indicator OK.
Check actual running hours of X-band/S-band radar magnetrons vs. manufacturer max limits.
All deck officers must demonstrate MF/HF DSC test calls (save the receipt).
Review passage plan to ensure it covers berth-to-berth, includes anchorage info, squat, and UKC.
"Zero Tide" Policy: Confirm HAT, HoT, and tide heights are set to zero during planning.
Fill in Safety Contour settings (Shallow, Safety, Deep) on ECDIS for waypoints. Navigation officers familiar with setup.
Confirm the pilot route plan matches the actual berth and sailing route. Identify No-Go areas.
Password for BNWAS is NOT posted next to system panel. BNWAS shows no DC alarm; fully functional.
7. Pollution Prevention & Galley
Conduct 15ppm separator alarming test; ensure auto-stop device activates within 20 seconds. Crew able to demonstrate.
Ensure the bypass OWS overboard discharge valve is physically "Close". Check sampling valve.
Never use portable pumps to pump bilge water into ballast tanks. Strictly follow Bunker Checklist.
Ensure sewage plant working (blower functioning); overboard valve fixed in "Close" position in port. Document discharge rate. Magic pipe not used.
Remark garbage bins per GMP. Ensure no garbage is stored outside designated stations. Containers are non-combustible.
Cargo Residue and cooking oil recorded in garbage record book. No excessive use of correction tape. Garbage placard posted.
Chief Cook must clean galley grease filters (free from oil). Exhaust pipe has NO excessive oil build up.
Position galley fire blanket in a noticeable spot. Keep galley door closed. Test exhaust trunk damper.
Galley, provision chambers maintained hygienic. Fish/meat chambers temperatures OK (not > -15 deg). Refrigeration alarm works. Sufficient food. Food not stored on floor.
Paint locker cleaned. Wire and ropes removed from locker. SDS and PPE available at storage.
Medicines organized and housekeeping inside Hospital room is good. Flushing system inside toilet/shower room works.
The contact list (ANNEX) in SOPEP is updated.

Area 9: Actual Detention Deficiencies (Real PSC Cases)

CRITICAL WARNING: The following items are historic PSC detention deficiencies (including severe new additions). Any failure in these items will almost certainly result in an immediate detention.

Actual Detention Deficiencies Detailed Functional Check & Actions Required Result Remarks
9.1 SMS, Documents & Personnel
  • Known severe damage to the main engine without risk assessment, reporting, and permanent repair per SMS.
 Conduct risk assessments & report critical equipment damage per SMS; implement temporary measures immediately.
  • No root cause analysis and preventive measures for historical deficiencies (e.g., OWS 15ppm, sewage high-level alarms).
 Ensure closed-loop corrective records for all historical deficiencies.
  • Key crew completely unfamiliar with the Shaft Power Limitation (ShaPoLi) system.
 Conduct practical drills and training for new systems like ShaPoLi.
  • Seafarers' Employment Agreement (SEA) missing contract expiry date and paid annual leave quota.
 Check all Seafarers' Employment Agreements (SEA) for exact expiry dates and annual leave quotas.
  • ECDIS familiarization training not conducted.
 Ensure ship-specific ECDIS familiarization training is completed and logged for all deck officers.
9.2 Engine Room & Machinery
  • Excessive oil mixture accumulated at the bottom of the E/R, stern tube drip tray, and purifier area.
 Thoroughly clean E/R bilges, stern tube drip trays, and purifier areas. Keep them dry.
  • Unapproved plastic buckets and rubber hoses used to drain oil.
 Strictly remove all unapproved plastic buckets and rubber hoses.
  • Main engine damaged since Dec 15 without permanent repair, reducing propulsion.
 Ensure any main/auxiliary engine damages have permanent repairs executed or Class conditions of authority present.
  • No. 2 generator lube oil massively leaking from pressure gauge.
 Repair all lube oil and fuel oil leaks on generators and main engine immediately.
  • Numerous engineroom pumps leaking (Fire GS, Bilge Ballast, Cooling SW, etc.)
 Inspect and repair mechanical seals and packings on all E/R pumps to ensure zero active leakage.
  • Steering gear alarm completely failed; emergency steering exceeded the 28s requirement.
 Test steering gear alarms and conduct an emergency steering time test (must be ≤ 28 seconds).
  • Main high-pressure fuel oil pipe jacket leak alarm and collection missing.
 Check high-pressure fuel pipe leak alarms and collection system.
  • Portable fuel oil sulfur content test exceeded 0.5% limit.
 Ensure in-use fuel sulfur content complies with MARPOL (<0.5%).
  • No. 2 ballast pump motor dismantled and not repaired.
 Repair defective ballast pumps or evaluate for official downgrade per SMS.
9.3 Fire Fighting Systems
  • Fire pump & emergency fire pump failed to generate sufficient pressure.
 Conduct a loaded running test for main and emergency fire pumps; record outlet pressure.
  • Emergency fire pump room flooded, insulation resistance at 0 Mega-ohms.
 Keep emergency pump room dry; test motor insulation to ensure it is >0 MΩ.
  • Cargo hold side fire main expansion joint leaking. Fire hoses leaking.
 Replace all leaking fire main joints and defective hoses.
  • Port and starboard engine room fan dampers defective. (SOLAS Ch.II-2 / Reg. 5.2.1)
 Inspect, lubricate, and test physical closure of all E/R fan dampers. Ensure tight seal.
  • Galley vent flap seized.
 Test galley exhaust vent flap mobility and grease moving parts.
  • CO2 system pressure abnormal/insufficient, door alarm limit switch stuck.
 Check CO2 cylinder pressures and test all door alarm limit switches.
  • Cargo hold fixed CO2 installed in 20ft container without permanent securing.
 Verify CO2 arrangement compliance and ensure it is permanently secured.
  • Water-based local application system intentionally shut off.
 Never intentionally shut off water-based systems. Ensure it is set to AUTOMATIC.
  • Paint locker nozzle blocked.
 Clear and unblock paint locker nozzles.
  • Unapproved penetrations in A-class fire boundaries.
 Check fire penetrations and ensure approved sealing is applied.
  • A-60 Class division of escape trunk is damaged... bottom door sill not insulated.
 Inspect A-60 insulation on escape trunks and door sills; repair any damage.
  • Emergency/escape route fire doors cannot close completely; self-closer failed. Fire doors tied open.
 Test fire door self-closers and remove ALL ropes/hooks holding doors open.
  • E/R escape route blocked.
 Clear E/R escape routes completely.
9.4 Life Saving Appliances
  • LB engines failed to start via batteries; STBD engine emitted heavy black smoke.
 Test dual battery engine starts and run engines to ensure smooth operation.
  • Embarkation ladder steps cracked. Davit limit switch failed, release gear not properly reset.
 Replace cracked ladder steps. Check davit limit switches and ensure release gear is correctly reset.
  • Rescue boat engine failed to start; hydraulic oil pipe burst during operation.
 Test rescue boat engine. Replace burst hydraulic pipes immediately.
  • Rescue Boat Launching Davit not operational.
 Test rescue boat davit on main power and dead-ship manual backup system.
  • Liferaft release arrangement stuck, embarkation route blocked.
 Clear liferaft embarkation routes and lubricate release mechanisms.
  • Life-saving appliances missing or expired. Two-way VHF batteries expired.
 Inventory and replace expired LSA and 2-way VHF batteries.
  • Emergency generator failed to start, losing key emergency power.
 Test emergency generator auto-start and load-sharing connection to switchboard.
  • Emergency lights at liferaft area failed to illuminate the water. Deck escape route emergency lights failed.
 Conduct night rounds to check all emergency lights, especially at LSA stations and exits.
9.5 Structure & Weathertightness
  • Cargo hold hatch weathertightness failed. Rubber packings aged, securing devices mostly failed.
 Replace aged hatch packings and defective securing cleats.
  • Coaming/hatch cover drain valves missing or stuck.
 Replace missing drain valves and clear any blocked drains.
  • Air pipes and ventilators severely rusted through, float balls stuck.
 Hammer-test air pipe heads, ensure float balls move freely. Repair rust.
  • Port and starboard W/T doors to bosun store defective and not watertight... securing dogs defective/missing.
 Inspect W/T doors to bosun store, replace missing dogs, conduct chalk/hose test.
  • Holes in hull structure and bulkheads. Deck and bulwark severely rusted/thinned.
 Inspect and repair hull holes and severe rust patches.
  • WT/WTD doors severely damaged, cannot close properly.
 Test all WT/WTD doors for proper closure and seal.
  • Freeboard marks/deck line blurred. Railings deformed.
 Repaint freeboard/deck lines to contrast color. Fix railings to meet height rules.
  • Heavy objects (steel ladders, pipes) in bosun store unfixed and randomly piled.
 Secure heavy objects in bosun store to prevent shifting.
9.6 Navigation Equipment
  • Safety settings (shallow contour, safety contour & safety depth) entered in ECDIS not in accordance with voyage plan.
 Cross-check ECDIS safety settings against the approved voyage plan. Correct any discrepancies.
  • Passage plan missing information, not covering "berth to berth", and not updated to anchorage route.
 Review passage plan to ensure it covers berth-to-berth and includes anchorage routing.
  • Passage plan waypoint table missing ship's squat information. ECDIS key information (e.g., UKC) missing or not updated.
 Ensure UKC and squat are calculated and entered in passage plan / ECDIS.
  • Radar/repeater heading not synchronized with master gyro compass. Wing rudder angle indicator defective.
 Synchronize radar/gyro headings. Test wing rudder indicators.
  • STBD side light casing cracked; fore/aft navigation lights failed. Port/STBD side light screens not painted in matt black.
 Replace damaged light casings. Inspect all lights. Paint screens matt black.
9.7 Pollution Prevention, Galley & ISPS
  • Oil Filtering Equipment (OWS) not operational and 15 ppm monitor could not be tested.
 Ensure OWS is fully operational and run 15ppm monitor test; ensure auto-stop activates <20s.
  • Improper ballast water management: portable pump used to transfer water to port ballast tank.
 Never use portable pumps to pump bilge water into ballast tanks.
  • Bunkering operations not following the "Bunker Checklist".
 Strictly follow Bunker Checklist during all operations.
  • Sewage treatment plant not operated as per manufacturers instructions.
 Operate sewage plant strictly per manufacturer's manual (check aeration/dosing).
  • Sewage treatment plant chemical dosing pipe connected wrongly, dosing pump failed. Untreated sewage discharge rate not provided.
 Repair sewage dosing pump and chemical lines. Document discharge rate.
  • Accommodation garbage bins not marked per GMP; Category A/C mismanaged.
 Remark garbage bins per GMP. Verify reception receipts.
  • Security drill not carried out after crew change over 25%.
 Conduct security drill if crew change >25%.
  • Ship access control not effectively maintained at gangway: visitor IDs/cards ignored. PSCO boarded directly without checking.
 Gangway watch rigidly checks IDs and issues visitor cards. No exceptions.

Immediate Actions on PSCO Boarding

ALERT
Action Protocols
  • ► Chief Officer (C/O): Immediately ensure cargo hold hatch covers are safely secured open. Ensure safety pins for mooring winches are secured in designated positions.
  • ► Chief Engineer (C/E): Immediately check the Engine Control Room (ECR) for active alarms. Verify OWS and Sewage overboard valves are strictly closed.
  • ► Engine Team: Double-check all Engine Room fire doors are completely closed (remove any ropes). Quickly wipe down fresh oil leaks and ensure escape routes are 100% clear.
  • ► Bridge Team: Ensure all navigation records, passage plans, and DSC receipts are ready on the chart table.
  • ► Chief Cook: Immediately close all self-closing fire doors in the galley. Check grease filters.
  • ► All Crew: Stop provisional delivery immediately until the PSCO disembarks. Do not leave operations unattended.
