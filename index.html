Inspection Report

This form is not a questionnaire. Walk to the sites where the devices and equipment are located and conduct functional testing right away.

Area 1: SMS, Documents & Personnel

Category Common Deficiencies
(常被開立之缺失)		 Detailed Functional Check & Actions Required Result Remarks

SMS & Records
  • Known severe damage to the main engine without risk assessment, reporting, and permanent repair per SMS.
  • No root cause analysis and preventive measures for historical deficiencies (e.g., OWS 15ppm, sewage high-level alarms).
 ■ Conduct risk assessments & report critical equipment damage per SMS; implement temporary measures.
■ Ensure closed-loop corrective records for all historical deficiencies.
■ Document and report any disorders related to critical equipment.
■ Ensure Critical Equipment records (WMC-20, 21), pre-work permits, Loading Plans, and BW logs are updated and available.

Crew Training & SEA
  • Key crew completely unfamiliar with the Shaft Power Limitation (ShaPoLi) system.
  • Seafarers' Employment Agreement (SEA) missing contract expiry date and paid annual leave quota.
  • ECDIS familiarization training not conducted.
 ■ Conduct practical drills for new systems like ShaPoLi.
■ Check all Seafarers' Employment Agreements (SEA) for expiry dates and annual leave quotas.
■ Ensure ECDIS familiarization training is completed and logged.

Rest Hours & Language

 General ISM & Operational verification. ■ Ensure crew's rest hour records exactly match actual activities.
■ Verify 15-minute fire patrols reflect on watch schedules and rest hour records.
■ Display "Working Language Is English" note in Deck and Engine log books.
■ Demonstrate effective English communication if crew is mixed nationalities.

Area 2: Engine Room & Machinery

Category Common Deficiencies
(常被開立之缺失)		 Detailed Functional Check & Actions Required Result Remarks

Oil Pollution Control
  • Excessive oil mixture accumulated at the bottom of the E/R, stern tube drip tray, and purifier area.
  • Unapproved plastic buckets and rubber hoses used to drain oil.
 ■ Thoroughly clean E/R bilges, stern tube drip trays, and purifier areas. Unblock purifier oil drain pipes.
■ Strictly remove all unapproved plastic buckets and rubber hoses.

ME, AE & Steering
  • Main engine damaged since Dec 15 without permanent repair, reducing propulsion.
  • No. 2 generator lube oil massively leaking from pressure gauge.
  • Steering gear alarm completely failed; emergency steering exceeded the 28s requirement.
 ■ Repair main and auxiliary engine oil leaks.
■ Test steering gear alarms and conduct an emergency steering time test (must be ≤ 28 seconds).
■ All engine crew must complete training and practice the emergency steering procedure.

Fuel, Ballast & Gauges
  • Main high-pressure fuel oil pipe jacket leak alarm and collection missing.
  • Portable fuel oil sulfur content test exceeded 0.5% limit.
  • No. 2 ballast pump motor dismantled and not repaired.
 ■ Check high-pressure fuel pipe leak alarms. Ensure in-use fuel sulfur content complies (<0.5%).
■ Repair defective ballast pumps or evaluate for downgrade.
■ All engine officers must practice obtaining the boiler water level in the gauge glass.
■ Ensure quick-closing devices for oil level gauges of oil tanks are secured with wires.

Area 3: Fire Fighting Systems

Category Common Deficiencies
(常被開立之缺失)		 Detailed Functional Check & Actions Required Result Remarks

Fire Pumps & Hoses
  • Fire pump & emergency fire pump failed to generate sufficient pressure.
  • Emergency fire pump room flooded, insulation resistance at 0 Mega-ohms.
  • Cargo hold side fire main expansion joint leaking. Fire hoses leaking.
 ■ Keep emergency pump room dry; motor insulation must be >0 MΩ.
■ Conduct a loaded running test for main/emergency fire pumps; record outlet pressure in MPa.
■ Replace leaking fire main joints and hoses. Test remote start of main fire pump.
■ Inspect fire hoses/boxes (no cracks); check nozzle gaskets are not aged, cracked, or wasted.

Fixed Extinguishing
  • CO2 system pressure abnormal/insufficient, door alarm limit switch stuck.
  • Cargo hold fixed CO2 installed in 20ft container without permanent securing.
  • Water-based local application system intentionally shut off.
 ■ Check CO2 cylinder pressure, door alarms, and arrangement compliance.
■ Safety pins MUST be removed from fixed CO2 systems. Crew familiarized with simulation release.
■ Never intentionally shut off water-based systems.
■ Test stopping of power ventilation from outside. Test quick-closing valves for ME/generators.

Boundaries & LSA
  • Emergency/escape route fire doors cannot close completely; self-closer failed.
  • Fire doors/fire dampers tied open with ropes. E/R escape route blocked.
 ■ Test all fire door self-closers and remove any ropes/hooks holding doors open.
■ Check fire penetrations and clear E/R escape routes. Record simulation escape from E/R with EEBD.
■ Mark open/close status on funnel fire dampers. Comprehensive inspection on E/R fan dampers.
■ Foam applicator ready in E/R. Ensure SCBA/EEBD fully charged.
■ Post correct fire control plan symbol at gangways and update crew list.

Area 4: Life Saving & Emergency

Category Common Deficiencies
(常被開立之缺失)		 Detailed Functional Check & Actions Required Result Remarks

Lifeboats & Rescue
  • LB engines failed to start via batteries; STBD engine emitted heavy black smoke.
  • Embarkation ladder steps cracked. Davit limit switch failed, release gear not properly reset.
 ■ Test dual battery engine starts and run engines. Replace burst hydraulic pipes and cracked ladder steps.
■ Verify LB cradle/davit has no cracks, limit switches work, and release gear is reset. Move davit.
■ Remove Fall Preventer Devices (FPDs) immediately before berthing.
■ Check hydrostatic interlocks (free to move). Check LB windows & seat belts.
■ Rescue boat davit operable in "dead ship condition" (manual backup). Manuals readable/illuminated.

Liferafts & Suits
  • Liferaft release arrangement stuck, embarkation route blocked.
  • Life-saving appliances missing or expired. Two-way VHF batteries expired.
 ■ Clear liferaft embarkation routes and lubricate release. Ensure HRU/liferaft annual service is valid.
■ Ensure portable lights are provided for the bow liferaft. Check retro-reflective tapes.
■ Inventory and replace expired LSA and 2-way VHF batteries.
■ Conduct immersion suit donning practice. Ensure suits fit correctly, no damage, zippers waxed.

Emergency Power
  • Emergency generator failed to start, losing key emergency power.
  • Emergency lights at liferaft area failed to illuminate the water.
 ■ Test emergency generator auto-start and load-sharing.
■ Ensure the diesel oil tank for the emergency generator is full or sufficient.
■ Confirm the emergency generator connects automatically to the switchboard.
■ Conduct night rounds to check all emergency lights.

Area 5: Structure & Safety Culture

Category Common Deficiencies
(常被開立之缺失)		 Detailed Functional Check & Actions Required Result Remarks

Hatch Covers & Vents
  • Cargo hold hatch weathertightness failed. Rubber packings aged, securing devices mostly failed.
  • Air pipes and ventilators severely rusted through, float balls stuck.
 ■ Replace aged hatch packings, drain valves, and securing bolts. Check coamings for corrosion.
■ Check all cleats; ensure rubber packing has no missing sections, deep cracks, or depressions.
■ Hammer-test air pipe heads and natural ventilators; ensure float balls move freely.
■ Inspect ventilation closing devices indicated on the Fire Control Plan.

Hull & Enclosed Spaces
  • Holes in hull structure and bulkheads. Deck and bulwark severely rusted/thinned.
  • WT/WTD doors severely damaged, cannot close properly.
 ■ Inspect/repair hull holes and severe rust; test WT/WTD doors. Repaint freeboard/deck lines.
■ Post the enclosed space list (WMR-OT-01) in mess rooms and offices.
■ Post "Unauthorized Entry" signage and apply appropriate locking methods to enclosed spaces.
■ Check gas detector calibration intervals (6/12 months), working order, and span gas availability.

PPE, Safety & Access
  • Heavy objects (steel ladders, pipes) in bosun store unfixed and randomly piled.
 ■ Ensure crew wears PPE; engine crew must wear hearing protection (notice posted at E/R entrance).
■ Verify fire curtains, insulation mats (switchboards), covered turbochargers, pump meshes in place.
■ Test the ship alarm and public addressing system. Secure heavy objects in bosun store.
■ Keep access clear: fire hydrants, bosun store extinguisher, CO2 room, anchor chain bitters.

Area 6: Navigation Equipment

Category Common Deficiencies
(常被開立之缺失)		 Detailed Functional Check & Actions Required Result Remarks

Lights & Instruments
  • STBD side light casing cracked; fore/aft navigation lights failed.
  • Radar/repeater heading not synchronized with master gyro compass. Wing rudder angle indicator defective.
 ■ Replace damaged light casings and paint side light screens matt black. Inspect all lights with housing.
■ Inspect Day Shapes (balls, cylinders, cones, diamonds) and measure their diameters.
■ Verify no bubbles in compasses; log observed errors for Gyro, Repeater, and Magnetic compasses.
■ Check actual running hours of X-band/S-band radar magnetrons vs. manufacturer max limits.
■ All deck officers must demonstrate MF/HF DSC test calls (save the receipt).

Passage Plan & ECDIS
  • Passage plan missing information, not covering "berth to berth", and not updated to anchorage route.
  • ECDIS key information (e.g., Under Keel Clearance - UKC) missing or not updated.
 ■ Review passage plan to ensure it covers berth-to-berth, includes anchorage info, squat, and UKC.
■ "Zero Tide" Policy: Confirm HAT, HoT, and tide heights are set to zero during planning.
■ Fill in Safety Contour settings (Shallow, Safety, Deep) on ECDIS for waypoints.
■ Confirm the pilot route plan matches the actual berth and sailing route. Identify No-Go areas.
■ Ensure paper copies of IAMSAR Vol III and Code of Signals are available.

Area 7 & 8: Pollution & Security

Category Common Deficiencies
(常被開立之缺失)		 Detailed Functional Check & Actions Required Result Remarks

OWS & Ballast
  • Oily Water Separator (OWS) 15ppm functional test could not be carried out.
  • Improper ballast water management: portable pump used to transfer water to port ballast tank.
 ■ Conduct 15ppm separator alarming test; ensure auto-stop device activates within 20 seconds.
■ Ensure the bypass OWS overboard discharge valve is physically "Close". Check sampling valve.
■ Never use portable pumps to pump bilge water into ballast tanks. Strictly follow Bunker Checklist.

Sewage & Galley
  • Accommodation garbage bins not marked per GMP; Category A/C mismanaged.
  • Sewage treatment plant chemical dosing pipe connected wrongly, dosing pump failed.
 ■ Ensure sewage plant working; overboard valve fixed in "Close" position in port. Document discharge rate.
■ Remark garbage bins per GMP. Ensure no garbage is stored outside designated stations.
■ Chief Cook must clean galley grease filters (free from oil).
■ Position galley fire blanket in a noticeable spot. Keep galley door closed. Test exhaust trunk damper.

ISPS & Alert Actions
  • Security drill not carried out after crew change over 25%.
  • PSCO boarded directly to the bridge without showing ID and without being checked.
 ■ Conduct security drill if crew change >25%. Gangway watch rigidly checks IDs and issues visitor cards.
■ Extract and post National contact points info ONLY for the upcoming port.
■ C/O: Ensure cargo hold hatch covers are safely secured open & mooring winch safety pins are secured.
■ Chief Cook: Close all self-closing fire doors in the galley immediately.
■ Stop provisional delivery immediately until PSCO disembarks.
